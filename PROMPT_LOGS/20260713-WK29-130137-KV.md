## Two AWS services, both called "EventBridge" — the newer one has timezones

| Service | CFN type | Timezone support | Launched |
|---------|----------|------------------|----------|
| **EventBridge Rules** (classic) | `AWS::Events::Rule` | UTC only | 2016 |
| **EventBridge Scheduler** (newer) | `AWS::Scheduler::Schedule` | **Yes — IANA timezones + auto DST** | Nov 2022 |

EventBridge Scheduler is exactly what you're remembering. It supports `ScheduleExpressionTimezone: America/Chicago` and handles DST transitions automatically — 2 AM local time year-round, no drift.

## Updated design

I'll use `AWS::Scheduler::Schedule` instead of the older `AWS::Events::Rule`. The CFN piece looks like:

```yaml
NightlyBackupSchedule:
  Type: AWS::Scheduler::Schedule
  Properties:
    Name: cf-migration-jumpbox-nightly-backup
    Description: Nightly backup of jumpbox state to sandbox S3
    ScheduleExpression: cron(0 2 * * ? *)
    ScheduleExpressionTimezone: America/Chicago         # ← the key line
    FlexibleTimeWindow:
      Mode: 'OFF'
    State: ENABLED
    Target:
      Arn: arn:aws:scheduler:::aws-sdk:ssm:sendCommand
      RoleArn: !GetAtt SchedulerExecutionRole.Arn
      Input: !Sub |
        {
          "DocumentName": "AWS-RunShellScript",
          "InstanceIds": ["${Jumpbox}"],
          "Parameters": {
            "commands": [
              "aws s3 cp s3://${MigrationBucketName}/scripts/jumpbox/backup-state.sh /tmp/",
              "chmod +x /tmp/backup-state.sh",
              "/tmp/backup-state.sh"
            ]
          },
          "TimeoutSeconds": 600
        }
```

Plus a small execution role that trusts `scheduler.amazonaws.com` and can `ssm:SendCommand` scoped to the specific jumpbox instance.

**Result:** Fires at 02:00 America/Chicago every day — 2 AM CST in winter, 2 AM CDT in summer. No annual "did DST just shift my backup window?" gotcha.

## Your reasoning is exactly right

> "backup be done as soon after data is created as possible"

Fully agree. If a jumpbox dies at 6 PM after a full day of operator activity, running the backup at 2 AM the *following* morning means only ~8 hours of change is potentially lost. Running it at 8 AM would mean 14 hours. Nightly-soon-after-midnight is the right window for a machine humans touch during the day.

## Small implementation detail

The jumpbox CFN template's parameters currently include `MigrationBucketArn` but not the bucket *name* directly. To inject into the scheduler's `Input`, we either:
- Add a `MigrationBucketName` parameter (cleaner)
- Extract the name from the ARN via `!Select [5, !Split [':', !Ref MigrationBucketArn]]` (slick but hard to read)

I'll go with the parameter — one line, no cognitive overhead.
