### 16:32:06 - Response

You're catching a real corner that my table phrasing glossed over. Two distinct things in play:

**Where ACM can vs. cannot deploy a cert:**

ACM-managed certs can attach to AWS-managed TLS terminators: **ALB, NLB (with TLS listener), CloudFront, API Gateway, App Runner, Cognito, App Mesh, Elastic Beanstalk**. They **cannot** be exported to `/etc/nginx/ssl/cert.pem` on an EC2 instance. The private key stays inside ACM's HSM-backed storage — it's never accessible to your OS. So:

- ✅ Our current architecture (TLS terminates at the ALB) — ACM works perfectly
- ❌ If we ever moved TLS termination back to nginx-on-EC2 — ACM is useless for that path

That's the gotcha. Most people don't notice because the modern AWS pattern *is* "terminate at ALB" — but if Worx's hosting model needs per-customer cert management on the nginx tier, ACM falls off the table.

**ALB cert limits (your memory is correct):**

| Limit | Default | Adjustable? |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Certs per ALB | **25** | Yes, via support ticket. Operationally usable up to ~100-500. |
| ALBs per region | 50 | Yes, by ticket |
| ACM certs per region | 2,500 | Yes, by ticket |

For Zoning Info specifically (1-5 sandboxes/staging/prod + dev clones), we're nowhere near any of those. **For Worx's multi-tenant hosting at "hundreds of sites," ALB with per-tenant certs is the wrong tool** — even with a quota raise, you're spreading sites across multiple ALBs ($16/mo each), routing rules get fiddly, and you don't scale to thousands without serious architectural fight.

**What Worx will likely need at scale (rough sketch):**

The right pattern for "hundreds-to-thousands of tenant sites, each with their own domain" is usually:

1. **NLB → nginx-on-EC2 with self-managed certs.** NLB passes TLS straight through to nginx (no termination at NLB). nginx terminates with per-domain certs managed by something like `lego`, `dehydrated`, or `certbot` against Let's Encrypt's ACME endpoint. nginx can hold **thousands** of certs per instance — RAM-bounded, not API-bounded. Renewal automation runs as a sidecar on each nginx host or in a central cert-controller. This is roughly what Cloudflare, Heroku, Fly.io, etc. do internally.

2. **CloudFront with SNI** for the CDN tier. CloudFront supports many SNI certs per distribution (and the dedicated-IP-SSL fee that used to make this expensive went away years ago). Good for CDN-style fronting, less good for per-tenant routing with origin variability.

3. **AWS Private CA / Private CA Connector for ACM** — different problem (private corporate trust), not applicable here.

4. **Commercial:** NGINX Plus, F5, Kong Gateway, etc. — license fees, but they handle 10k+ certs per instance and have built-in ACME clients.

For Worx, my expectation is **option 1**. Not free — you replace ACM's auto-renewal with your own renewal infrastructure — but it's the only path that scales to "I just signed a contract with customer #387 and they want their domain live by Friday."

**My recommendation for what to do right now:**

1. **Proceed with ACM-on-ALB for Zoning Info.** It's the right call at this scale.
2. **Capture the scale-limit constraint as a memory file** so future-us (or future-Worx-implementation) doesn't blindly copy this architecture to a hundred-tenant project. Proposed file: `docs/memory/tls-cert-strategy-by-scale.md`. Contents: the comparison above, the ALB limits, the "when ACM stops being the right answer" threshold, and a sketch of the NLB+nginx+ACME-client pattern for the multi-tenant case.
