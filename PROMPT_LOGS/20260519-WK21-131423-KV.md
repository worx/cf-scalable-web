### 13:15:43 - Response

You're exactly right — `validate` is a prereq of every `deploy-*` target and validates **all 11 templates + every param file** each time, regardless of which stack is actually being deployed. In `deploy-all` it runs ~8 times and in `deploy-allX` more like ~10, so you're spending 100+ lines on duplicated "Validating X..." output.

Your env var idea is the right pattern. Cleanest implementation:

1. **Add a 4-line guard at the top of `validate:`** — if `$$VALIDATED = "1"`, echo `(already validated, skipping)` and exit 0.
2. **`deploy-all` calls `validate` once explicitly at the top**, then invokes each sub-target with `VALIDATED=1` passed as an override.
3. **Make's recursive-invocation propagation** carries `VALIDATED=1` through `deploy-compute` → `deploy-compute-{alb,nlb,nginx,php}` automatically, so no per-target edits needed beyond the top-level compound targets (deploy-all, deploy-allX).

The tradeoff is tiny: if you edit a template *during* a long deploy-all run, the new edit isn't re-validated. In practice you don't edit mid-deploy, so it's a non-issue.

**Bonus suggestion while we're in there:** compress `validate`'s output from per-template chatter (~15 lines) to a single "✓ 11 templates + 6 param files valid" line, silencing the per-item lines unless one fails. Combined with the once-per-run change, deploy-all goes from ~150 lines of validation noise to 1-2 lines.
