### 18:14:56 - Response

Good point. A running `make deploy-all` doesn't need an SSM session — it's running in tmux. And CPU-based alarms won't work either because the instance is mostly idle between CloudFormation polling loops (sleeping 30-60 seconds, checking status, sleeping again).

The most reliable signal is actually simpler: **is tmux running any sessions?** If you always work in tmux, then:
- tmux sessions exist → something is running or you're coming back
- no tmux sessions → nothing happening, safe to stop

But even that's imperfect. You might finish a deploy, exit tmux, and want to poke around for 10 minutes before disconnecting.

Honestly, the cleanest approach might be the dumbest: just add `make stop-deploy-host` to your workflow. You're already running `make deploy-all` from it. When you check back and everything's done, `make stop-deploy-host` from your laptop. $1.60/month for the idle disk. Forgetting to stop it for a full month is only ~$6 in compute — hardly worth engineering an auto-stop system to prevent.

Want to just leave it manual, or still want to pursue the auto-stop?
