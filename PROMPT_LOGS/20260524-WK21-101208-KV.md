```
perf(orchestrators): split cmp into 4 sub-tracks; hoist cch/db to T=0 in destroy

Two related optimizations to both parallel orchestrators, motivated by
Kurt's first destroy-allXX run (23m45s) where cmp took 13m19s
sequential and cch/db were unnecessarily blocked waiting on it.

1. Split `cmp` into 4 sub-tracks (alb, nlb, cnx, cph).

   The single cmp track was deploying/destroying ALB → NLB → nginx → PHP
   sequentially internally. Splitting exposes the parallelism:

   DEPLOY:
     alb deps=[vpc]                           ⎫  parallel after VPC
     nlb deps=[vpc]                           ⎭
     cnx deps=[alb, ami, db, cch, str, ...]   ⎫  parallel after their LBs
     cph deps=[nlb, ami, db, cch, str, ...]   ⎭  + everything else ready

     cmp wall-clock today: ~7 min (sequential)
     After split:          ~4 min (max(alb,nlb)=1 + max(cnx,cph)=3)

   DESTROY:
     cnx deps=[]                              ⎫  parallel at T=0
     cph deps=[]                              ⎭
     alb deps=[cnx]                           ⎫  parallel after their ASGs gone
     nlb deps=[cph]                           ⎭

     cmp wall-clock today: ~13 min (sequential)
     After split:          ~6-8 min (max(cnx,cph)=4 + max(alb,nlb)=3)

   Splits are safe per CFN imports: nginx imports from cf-compute-alb,
   PHP imports from cf-compute-nlb. ALB and NLB are mutually independent.
   nginx and PHP are mutually independent.

2. Hoist cch and db to T=0 in destroy.

   They had deps=["cmp"] but there's no CFN import from compute → cache/
   database. The dependency was operator-side conservatism — wait for
   compute gone before touching cache. At the CFN level both stacks are
   completely independent of compute.

   Hoisting them to T=0 (no deps) lets them run in parallel with
   compute destroy. In Kurt's run, db took 7m9s starting at 13m19s →
   finishing at 20m28s. Starting at T=0 instead, db finishes at ~7m,
   well under the compute long pole. ~7 min saved on db's contribution
   to total wall-clock.

   Storage still depends on compute (FSx mounts on nginx/PHP must release
   first) — that one stays sequential.

Combined expected savings (destroy): ~5-7 min off the ~23 min baseline.
Combined expected savings (deploy):  ~3 min off the ~33 min baseline.

Also bundled `make update-ami-params` into the deploy `ami` track's cmd
(was previously inside `cmp`). Now downstream tracks depend on `ami`
for both AMI-bake AND SSM-AMI-IDs availability. Cleaner.

Track counts:
  deploy-all-parallel: 11 → 14 tracks  (cmp → alb + nlb + cnx + cph)
  destroy-all-parallel: 9 → 12 tracks  (cmp → cnx + cph + alb + nlb)

Display still fits comfortably in any modern terminal — adds ~3 lines
to the live block.

Files Modified:
```
